Recall-risk inventories

These are complete deterministic inventories, not statistical recall estimates. Human labels are required before estimating statistical recall.

MRI separation. We track three separate things: (1) the label says MRI-safe, (2) the item actually conflicts with imaging systems, and (3) it is a surgical device used around imaging. So “MRI-compatible / conditional / safe” language is inventoried separately from actual imaging equipment and from perioperative surgical-device evidence. The later S12 guard never promotes a row in this release.

Pakistan nonstandard manufacturer and HS-prior tiers — records with unusual formatting, or matched only via an older HS-code prior — remain reviewable and are never relabeled Trusted by this audit. MRI and nonstandard recommendations are shadow-only.

Reviewer-ready sample

Exact design

25 rows per output: 12 hand-picked target rows plus 13 rows chosen by a fixed algorithm for even coverage across data groups (a deterministic stratified-random sample), for 150 total. The seed is 20260710.

Responsible inference

Random rows carry inclusion probability and sampling weight. Purposeful targets do not support weighted population estimates. Surgical relevance and mapping correctness are labeled separately.

Precision compliance means the logic ran correctly (deterministic rules compliance), not statistical precision. Recall proxy means we listed every potential risk by rule (complete deterministic risk coverage), not statistical recall — human labels are still needed.
